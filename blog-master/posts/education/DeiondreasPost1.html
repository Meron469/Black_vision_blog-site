Cant Afford a Financial Advisor/Planner? 4 books to advise you instead.

  • Deiondrea Berry
  • 20 April 2020
  • 3
  • 241
Share Tweet Pin Email

With the 1.5 trillion federal student loan debt and starving wages, it's hard to find funds for a financial advisor. Financial Advisors can cost a $2,000 flat fee just to initiate talks, hourly fees ($100 - $400) for each discussion, and then they'll take a small percentage (~1%) of your assets. Thanks to advances in technology, robo-advisors have become extremely common and affordable. They only charge low-cost management fees. But something else is extremely important when considering hiring an advisor; It is to actually understand money yourself so that you dont get led astray or ripped off. In the end, you will be able to decide what's best for you even though it's easier to dish it off to a computer. Below I have compiled a list of 4 books that may fit your situation. Click the book title to purchase the book on Amazon. Underneath the first two books I give a summary, so see if any of these books fit your needs. The last two books are similiar so i give a combined explanation above them.

  1. The Index Card: Why Personal Finance Doesn't Have to Be Complicated



    This book is my personal favorite. It's at number 1 for a reason. It's for those completely lost when it comes to money. It teaches concepts through the stories of fictional characters. One of the best ways to make less mistakes is to see others make mistakes and learn from them. The less you know about sound money management, the more valuable this book will prove to be. Literally. Very few people, regardless of income level, understand every aspect that contributes to maximizing (or jeopardizing) one's financial health. The authors claim that by following the nine simple rules as outlined on our index card, you will have the confidence to make your own financial decisions, discover basic financial truths such as that low-fee index funds outperform just about any more complicated investment you can buy and that simple fixed-rate mortgages remain the best way to borrow money to buy your house, be armed with a timeless set of guidelines that you can turn to no matter what financial issues you may face or how drastically the winds of financial change shift, and be sure you never make the same mistake Sam made and let your fears about the financial unknown prevent you from doing anything. Financial health is quite important and this book can be your doctor.

  2. Get a Financial Life: Personal Finance in Your Twenties and Thirties



    If you happen to be in your 20s and 30s and made it this far in this article, Congratulations. This book will transform your mindset and your financial situation.

    [This book] took me from 29 year old man child to a surprisingly competent adult. If that's not a glowing endorsement I don't know what is. Recommended reading after you complete this is "Bogleheads Guide to Investing": similar investment philosophy, more depth on how to go from just understanding the concepts to building a diversified, low cost, tax efficient portfolio.

    Vikramaditya
    It specifically targets the younger demographic but is beneficial to all. It takes you through everything step by step without the bore. Beware this book applies to individuals in the United States. The impact is diminished if you live elsewhere. Besides that, it is a simple easy to read book that deserves your time.

    3. Financial Independence Section

    The next two books are focused on financial independence ie. not having to work for money. Some people dont like working to make someone richer just to feed, clothe, and house themselves. While this may be an extreme case, some people simply dont like working for anyone else. These two books provide guidelines to becoming financially independent. The former tells you how to go about the process while the latter changes your mindset about money and your life to help you achieve financial independence.

  3. The Simple Path to Wealth: Your road map to financial independence and a rich, free life


  4. Your Money or Your Life: 9 Steps to Transforming Your Relationship with Money and Achieving Financial Independence
